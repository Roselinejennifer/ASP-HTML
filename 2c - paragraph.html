Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and passed away on July 27, 2015.

Early Life and Education

Dr. Kalam was born into a Tamil Muslim family. His father was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque, and his mother was a housewife. Despite his humble beginnings, Kalam went on to study physics and aerospace engineering.

He graduated from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later attended the Madras Institute of Technology, where he specialized in aerospace engineering.

Career as a Scientist

Dr. Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist after graduating. He later moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he was a project director of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) which deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980.

Known as the Missile Man of India, Kalam played a pivotal role in the development of the country's missile and nuclear weapons programs, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

Presidency and Legacy

Dr. Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India in 2002 with the support of both the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party and the opposition Indian National Congress. During his presidency, he was affectionately known as the "People's President" for his down-to-earth approach and efforts to reach out to the common people, especially the youth.

After his term as President, Kalam remained active as a visiting professor at several prestigious institutions and continued to inspire millions through his books and speeches. His vision for India's development was encapsulated in his famous work "India 2020," which outlined his dream of transforming India into a developed nation by the year 2020.

Honors and Awards

Throughout his life, Dr. Kalam received numerous awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997. He was also the recipient of the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan.

His contributions to science and technology, as well as his inspirational role as a leader and educator, continue to be remembered and celebrated by people all over the world.
